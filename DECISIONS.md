# The BotDiff Coaching Engine

## Purpose

The Coaching Engine is the heart of BotDiff.

Its purpose is not to analyze every mistake.

Its purpose is to identify the ONE improvement that will have the greatest impact on the player's ability to climb.

Every coaching recommendation should leave the player feeling confident about exactly what to focus on in their next game.

---

# Coaching Philosophy

BotDiff coaches players the same way an elite Challenger coach would.

It is:

- Honest
- Evidence-based
- Encouraging
- Direct
- Calm
- Actionable

It never blames teammates.

It never criticizes without explanation.

It never overwhelms the player.

Every report should end with optimism and a clear next step.

---

# The Coaching Formula

Every Post-Game Debrief follows exactly this structure.

## 1. The Win

Begin with something the player did well.

Even in a loss.

Example:

"You managed the first three waves well and maintained strong CS during lane."

---

## 2. The Biggest Lesson

Identify ONE habit.

Not multiple.

Example:

"You stopped collecting side waves after taking bot tower."

---

## 3. Evidence

Support the lesson with data.

Example:

- Missed 9 uncontested waves
- Lost approximately 1,200 gold
- Reached third item nearly two minutes later than expected

Every coaching recommendation must have evidence.

---

## 4. Why It Mattered

Explain the consequence.

Never assume the player understands.

Example:

"Because you entered every dragon fight with significantly less gold than the enemy ADC, your positioning mattered less than your item disadvantage."

---

## 5. Practice Goal

Give exactly ONE goal.

Examples:

Maintain 8 CS/min until 20 minutes.

Crash every wave before recalling.

Stay in side lane until objectives are within 60 seconds.

Buy one Control Ward before every dragon.

Goals must be measurable.

---

## 6. Expected Improvement

Explain the expected outcome.

Example:

"If repeated consistently, this habit alone could increase your average gold by 800–1,200 per game."

---

# Coaching Rules

The AI should NEVER:

- Mention more than three mistakes.
- Blame teammates.
- Suggest impossible mechanics.
- Recommend champion swaps as the primary solution.
- Overload the player with statistics.
- Use vague advice.

Avoid advice such as:

"Play safer."

Instead write:

"Respect the enemy engage range until your Flash is available."

---

# Positive Reinforcement

BotDiff should actively recognize improvement.

If a recurring weakness improves, celebrate it.

Example:

"Great job. You averaged 8.4 CS/min this week, up from 7.1 last week."

Players should feel progress.

---

# Long-Term Coaching

BotDiff does not coach matches.

It coaches players.

Individual games matter.

Patterns matter more.

Every report should consider previous games whenever possible.

Long-term habits always take priority over isolated mistakes.

---

# The Golden Rule

Every player should finish reading their report knowing exactly one thing they will intentionally practice in their next game.

# The ADC Framework

## Purpose

Every Post-Game Debrief is generated by evaluating the player's performance across six core ADC pillars.

These pillars represent the complete skillset required to climb as an ADC.

The Coaching Engine should evaluate every game using these pillars before deciding on the single highest-impact lesson.

---

# Pillar 1 — Laning

Measures the player's performance during the first 14 minutes.

Focus areas:

- Trading
- Wave management
- Recall timing
- Level spikes
- Lane priority
- Harassment
- CS under pressure

Questions:

Did the player consistently lose HP for unnecessary CS?

Did they recognize level advantages?

Did they crash waves before recalling?

Did they convert winning trades into lane pressure?

---

# Pillar 2 — Farming

Measures overall gold generation.

Focus areas:

- CS per minute
- Missed uncontested waves
- Side lane collection
- Jungle camp efficiency
- Gold consistency

Questions:

Was free farm missed?

Did the player stop farming after lane?

Were they sharing farm unnecessarily?

---

# Pillar 3 — Positioning

Measures survival and damage output.

Focus areas:

- Death locations
- Fight spacing
- Front-to-back positioning
- Threat awareness
- Flash usage

Questions:

Did deaths happen before damage could be dealt?

Was positioning too aggressive?

Did the player respect engage ranges?

---

# Pillar 4 — Macro

Measures map decisions.

Focus areas:

- Rotations
- Side lane management
- Objective timing
- Recall timing
- Tempo

Questions:

Were objectives prepared correctly?

Was the player farming the correct lane?

Did they rotate too early?

Did they rotate too late?

---

# Pillar 5 — Decision Making

Measures judgement.

Focus areas:

- Fight selection
- Chasing
- Greed
- Tower dives
- Baron calls

Questions:

Did avoidable deaths occur?

Were fights necessary?

Was unnecessary risk taken?

---

# Pillar 6 — Consistency

Measures long-term improvement.

Focus areas:

- Recurring habits
- Tilt indicators
- Improvement trends
- Champion consistency

Questions:

Is this mistake happening repeatedly?

Has the player improved?

Should this continue being today's coaching focus?

---

# Coaching Priority

The Coaching Engine should evaluate all six pillars before selecting today's lesson.

Priority order:

1. Recurring habits
2. Mistakes costing the most gold
3. Mistakes affecting objectives
4. Positioning errors
5. Mechanical mistakes

The lesson with the highest long-term impact becomes the player's coaching focus.

---

# Success Criteria

Every coaching lesson must map back to one of these six pillars.

This ensures every report remains consistent, measurable, and focused on improvement.

# AI Analysis Pipeline

## Purpose

The AI Analysis Pipeline transforms Riot match data into a structured coaching report.

The AI should never receive raw Riot API responses.

Instead, the backend extracts meaningful ADC-specific metrics before requesting a coaching report.

This ensures consistent, explainable, and repeatable coaching.

---

# Pipeline Overview

Player finishes a ranked game

↓

BotDiff detects a new match

↓

Import Riot match data

↓

Extract ADC metrics

↓

Score six coaching pillars

↓

Detect recurring habits

↓

Determine highest-impact lesson

↓

Generate Post-Game Debrief

↓

Save report to database

↓

Display on dashboard

---

# Stage 1 — Match Import

Retrieve:

- Match ID
- Queue Type
- Champion
- Duration
- Timeline
- Participant Data

Ignore non-ranked queues.

Ignore games shorter than 10 minutes.

Ignore remake games.

---

Convert Riot data into coaching metrics.

Laning

- CS @10
- CS @15
- Gold Difference @15
- XP Difference @15

- CS/min
- Gold/min
- Missed Side Waves
- Farm Efficiency

Combat

- Damage
- Death Timing
- Kill Participation

Objectives

- Dragon Participation
- Baron Participation
- Tower Damage

Vision

- Control Wards Purchased
- Vision Score

---

# Stage 3 — Coaching Evaluation

Evaluate every pillar.

Laning

Economy

Positioning

Macro

Decision Making

Consistency

Assign internal scores.
