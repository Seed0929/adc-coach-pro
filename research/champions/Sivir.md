# Sivir

Champion: Sivir

Class: Utility Hypercarry

Difficulty: Easy

Lane Phase: Average

Scaling: Excellent

Teamfighting: Excellent

Wave Clear: Elite

Mobility: Medium

Primary Damage: Sustained Physical

Coach Priority:

- Wave Management
- Positioning
- Spell Shield Usage
- Objective Control

---

# Champion Identity

Sivir is a scaling marksman who excels at controlling waves, rotating quickly around the map, and enabling her team with On The Hunt (R). She wins by creating tempo advantages through efficient farming and superior objective setup rather than dominating lane.

---

# Strengths

- Exceptional wave clear.
- Safe farming.
- Excellent late-game DPS.
- Powerful team movement with Ultimate.
- Strong objective control.
- Difficult to punish with proper Spell Shield usage.

---

# Weaknesses

- Short attack range.
- Weak early all-ins.
- Requires excellent positioning.
- Spell Shield has a long cooldown.
- Can struggle against heavy poke.

---

# Win Condition

Farm efficiently.

Push waves before objectives.

Reach core item spikes.

Use On The Hunt to create favorable fights.

---

# Lose Condition

Falling behind in CS.

Wasting Spell Shield.

Fighting before scaling.

Poor wave management.

---

# Laning Priorities

- Farm consistently.
- Push waves when safe.
- Use Boomerang Blade (Q) to punish grouped enemies.
- Save Spell Shield for important crowd control.
- Crash waves before recalling.

---

# Mid Game

Push side waves.

Rotate early to Dragons and Baron.

Use wave clear to maintain tempo.

Group when objectives are spawning.

---

# Late Game

Stay behind the frontline.

Attack the closest safe target.

Use Ultimate to begin or disengage from fights.

Prioritize survival over chasing kills.

---

# Common Mistakes

- Using Spell Shield on low-impact abilities.
- Ignoring side waves.
- Fighting instead of farming.
- Standing too close to enemy engage.
- Poor Ultimate timing.

---

# Coaching Focus

BotDiff should evaluate:

- Wave management.
- CS per minute.
- Spell Shield efficiency.
- Objective participation.
- Positioning.
- Teamfight survival.

---

# Mechanical Benchmarks

Strong Sivir players generally:

- Maintain 8+ CS/min.
- Consistently arrive first to objectives.
- Block high-impact abilities with Spell Shield.
- Use Ultimate to secure favorable fights or disengages.
- Convert wave pressure into map control.

---

# Power Spikes

Level 5

- Stronger wave clear with additional ranks in Ricochet.

Level 6

- On The Hunt enables aggressive rotations and engages.

One Item

- Excellent wave control and sustained damage.

Two Items

- Strong teamfight presence.

Three Items

- Elite late-game carry capable of controlling extended fights.

---

# Threat Checklist

Before every fight ask:

- Which crowd control ability should I save Spell Shield for?
- Have I pushed the nearest wave?
- Is Ultimate available?
- Am I positioned behind my frontline?
- Can I safely attack the closest target?

---

# Practice Goals

Example goals:

- Stay above 8 CS/min.
- Block at least one major crowd control ability with Spell Shield each game.
- Push the wave before every Dragon spawn.
- Arrive at every major objective before the enemy ADC.
