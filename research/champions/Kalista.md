# Kalista

Champion: Kalista

Class: Early Game Skirmisher

Difficulty: Very Hard

Lane Phase: Elite

Scaling: Average

Teamfighting: Good

Wave Clear: Good

Mobility: Elite

Primary Damage: Sustained Physical

Coach Priority:

- Objective Control
- Early Tempo
- Positioning
- Ultimate Coordination

---

# Champion Identity

Kalista is an aggressive early-game marksman who dominates skirmishes through mobility, objective control, and close coordination with her support. She excels at securing Dragons and Rift Heralds with Rend (E) while creating early map pressure.

Kalista wins games by building an early advantage and converting it into neutral objectives before enemy hypercarries outscale her.

---

# Strengths

- Exceptional early-game skirmishing.
- Elite mobility while attacking.
- Secure objective control with Rend.
- Strong all-in potential.
- Excellent lane pressure.
- Powerful support synergy through Fate's Call (R).

---

# Weaknesses

- Falls off in very late game.
- Short attack range.
- Extremely difficult mechanically.
- Relies heavily on support coordination.
- Vulnerable when isolated.

---

# Win Condition

Win lane.

Secure every early Dragon possible.

Snowball through objective control.

End the game before late-game carries fully scale.

---

# Lose Condition

Passive laning.

Missing early objective opportunities.

Poor Ultimate coordination.

Allowing the game to stall.

---

# Laning Priorities

- Fight around early level advantages.
- Build wave priority.
- Trade aggressively with support.
- Push before objectives.
- Track the enemy jungler.

---

# Mid Game

Control Dragons and Herald.

Rotate with teammates.

Pressure side lanes safely.

Use Fate's Call to create favorable engages or save allies.

---

# Late Game

Stay with your team.

Attack the closest safe target.

Use mobility to kite continuously.

Save Ultimate for high-impact moments.

---

# Common Mistakes

- Fighting without support nearby.
- Using Fate's Call too late.
- Ignoring objective timers.
- Overextending after winning lane.
- Trying to duel stronger late-game carries.

---

# Coaching Focus

BotDiff should evaluate:

- Dragon participation.
- Objective secure rate.
- Early-game lead.
- Positioning.
- Ultimate effectiveness.
- Death timing.

---

# Mechanical Benchmarks

Strong Kalista players generally:

- Secure early Dragons consistently.
- Build lane advantages before 15 minutes.
- Coordinate Ultimate effectively with support.
- Maintain constant movement during fights.
- Convert early leads into towers and objectives.

---

# Power Spikes

Level 2

- Elite all-in potential with support.

Level 6

- Fate's Call unlocks powerful engage and peel options.

One Item

- Excellent skirmishing power.

Two Items

- Strong objective control and sustained damage.

Three Items

- Still effective, but should rely on earlier advantages rather than scaling.

---

# Threat Checklist

Before every fight ask:

- Is my support close enough to use Fate's Call?
- Are we fighting around an objective?
- Can I kite safely?
- Which enemy crowd control ability is the biggest threat?
- Can we convert this fight into a Dragon, Baron, or tower?

---

# Practice Goals

Example goals:

- Secure every contested early Dragon.
- Build lane priority before every objective.
- Use Fate's Call to save or engage with your support effectively.
- Convert every won fight into an objective.
