# Draven

Champion: Draven

Class: Snowball Marksman

Difficulty: Very Hard

Lane Phase: Elite

Scaling: Good

Teamfighting: Good

Wave Clear: Good

Mobility: Low

Primary Damage: Burst Physical

Coach Priority:

- Aggression
- Axe Management
- Death Timing
- Snowball Conversion

---

# Champion Identity

Draven is an early-game snowball marksman who creates overwhelming gold leads through aggressive trading and cashing in League of Draven (Passive).

Unlike most ADCs, Draven is rewarded for taking calculated risks early, but punished heavily for dying before cashing in his passive.

A successful Draven player turns lane advantages into dragons, towers, and fast victories.

---

# Strengths

- Highest early-game damage among ADCs.
- Exceptional lane bullying.
- Massive gold generation through Passive.
- Strong all-in potential.
- Dominates early skirmishes.
- Can end games before enemy carries scale.

---

# Weaknesses

- Extremely punishing when behind.
- Requires excellent axe management.
- Vulnerable after dying with passive stacks.
- Low mobility.
- Difficult positioning during teamfights.

---

# Win Condition

Dominate lane.

Cash in passive stacks early.

Take first tower.

Secure early Dragons.

End the game before enemy hypercarries reach full build.

---

# Lose Condition

Dying before cashing in passive.

Playing passively.

Missing spinning axes.

Giving shutdowns.

Allowing the game to stall into late game.

---

# Laning Priorities

- Trade aggressively around spinning axes.
- Build passive stacks safely.
- Crash waves before recalling.
- Coordinate all-ins with support.
- Pressure enemy under tower.

---

# Mid Game

Push advantages.

Force objectives.

Look for skirmishes.

Continue snowballing gold lead.

Avoid unnecessary deaths with bounty.

---

# Late Game

Position carefully.

Attack the closest safe target.

Do not force risky plays because of early lead.

Respect enemy engage.

---

# Common Mistakes

- Fighting with low health to protect passive stacks.
- Chasing axes into dangerous positions.
- Playing too aggressively without vision.
- Holding a large bounty unnecessarily.
- Ignoring objectives after winning lane.

---

# Coaching Focus

BotDiff should evaluate:

- Passive cash-in timing.
- Axe management.
- Lane dominance.
- Death timing.
- Objective conversion.
- Positioning.

---

# Mechanical Benchmarks

Strong Draven players generally:

- Cash in passive before dying.
- Build consistent CS leads.
- Convert lane wins into Dragons and towers.
- Rarely lose lane priority.
- Avoid unnecessary bounty deaths.

---

# Power Spikes

Level 1

- One of the strongest champions in the game with Spinning Axe.

Level 2

- Massive all-in threat.

Level 6

- Whirling Death provides strong execute and cross-map pressure.

One Item

- Excellent burst damage.

Two Items

- Dominates mid-game skirmishes.

Three Items

- Strong carry, but relies more on positioning than scaling.

---

# Threat Checklist

Before every fight ask:

- How many passive stacks am I holding?
- Can I safely catch this axe?
- Do I have vision of the enemy jungler?
- Am I risking a shutdown?
- Will this fight help me snowball the game?

---

# Practice Goals

Example goals:

- Cash in passive before 15 minutes.
- Build a 15+ CS lead by 10 minutes.
- Do not die with more than 100 passive stacks.
- Convert every lane kill into a plate, tower, or Dragon.
