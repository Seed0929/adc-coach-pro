# ADC Framework

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

The ADC Framework is the coaching foundation of BotDiff.

Its purpose is to define what excellent ADC gameplay looks like and how every match should be evaluated.

BotDiff is not trying to identify every mistake.

BotDiff identifies the highest-impact habits preventing a player from climbing.

Every coaching decision must reference this framework.

---

# Core Philosophy

ADC is the role that converts resources into wins.

The best ADCs consistently:

• Farm efficiently
• Stay alive
• Position correctly
• Arrive prepared for objectives
• Make repeatable high-value decisions

Mechanics matter.

Decision making matters more.

---

# Coaching Priorities

BotDiff always evaluates matches in this order.

1. Positioning
2. Deaths
3. Wave Management
4. Recall Timing
5. Objective Setup
6. Farming
7. Teamfighting
8. Trading
9. Vision
10. Tempo

Earlier categories carry more weight.

---

# Skill Categories

## 1. Positioning

Weight

20%

Definition

The ability to maximize damage while minimizing unnecessary risk.

Excellent

• Maintains maximum attack range.
• Respects engage cooldowns.
• Uses frontline correctly.
• Rarely dies first.

Poor

• Walks into engage range.
• Chases kills.
• Face-checks.
• Dies before fights begin.

Coach Focus

Positioning is usually the highest LP-impact skill.

---

## 2. Laning

Weight

15%

Definition

The ability to generate advantages during the first 14 minutes.

Excellent

• Controls wave state.
• Wins priority.
• Trades efficiently.
• Punishes mistakes.

Poor

• Gives free pushes.
• Randomly fights.
• Misses opportunities.

---

## 3. CS Efficiency

Weight

15%

Definition

Converting lane time into gold.

Excellent

8–10 CS/min

Average

6–8 CS/min

Poor

Below 6 CS/min

Coach Focus

Gold wins games.

Missed farm compounds over time.

---

## 4. Wave Management

Weight

10%

Excellent

• Freezes correctly.

• Crashes before recalling.

• Slow pushes intentionally.

Poor

• Random pushing.

• Breaking freezes.

• Recalling on bad wave states.

---

## 5. Recall Timing

Weight

10%

Excellent

• Recall after crash.

• Recall before objectives.

• Efficient purchases.

Poor

• Greedy recalls.

• Staying with 2,000 gold.

• Missing dragon timers.

---

## 6. Teamfighting

Weight

15%

Excellent

• Hits safest target.

• Uses spacing.

• Maximizes uptime.

Poor

• Tunnel vision.

• Chasing backline.

• Walking forward unnecessarily.

---

## 7. Objective Setup

Weight

5%

Excellent

• Pushes waves first.

• Arrives early.

• Maintains positioning.

Poor

• Late rotations.

• Fighting before setup.

---

## 8. Trading

Weight

3%

Excellent

• Uses cooldown windows.

• Trades around minions.

Poor

• Fighting into stacked waves.

• Poor spacing.

---

## 9. Vision

Weight

5%

Excellent

• Wards proactively.

• Avoids face checks.

Poor

• Never wards.

• Walks into fog.

---

## 10. Tempo

Weight

2%

Definition

The efficient use of time.

Excellent

• Always farming.

• Always moving with purpose.

Poor

• Standing idle.

• Random roaming.

• Missed side waves.

# Grade Philosophy

Grades represent decision quality.

Not mechanics.

S+

Near-perfect decision making.

S

Exceptional.

A

Strong fundamentals.

B

Good.

C

Average.

D

Repeated costly mistakes.

F

Fundamental weaknesses.

Rules

Highest LP impact.

Recurring.

Fixable.

Evidence supported.

# Biggest Strength

Every report reinforces one positive habit.

Improvement comes from building strengths as well as fixing weaknesses.

# Win Condition

Every report ends with a personalized win condition.

Example

"Reach 8 CS/min while avoiding deaths before second dragon."

# Success

A player should finish every report knowing:

• Why they won or lost.

• What habit matters most.

• Why that habit matters.

• What to practice next game.
